Tim Hortons Hockey Challenge – Optimal Strategy Guide

This challenge combines three competing objectives:

  • Daily expected points (EV)
  • Hot streak probability (≥1 correct for 7 days)
  • Leaderboard ceiling (maximize total points)

Key insight: There is no single optimal strategy. The best approach depends on your goal.

1. Core Probability Model

Let each pick have probabilities: p₁, p₂, p₃

  • P(0 hits) = (1−p₁)(1−p₂)(1−p₃)
  • P(≥1 hit) = 1 − P(0 hits)
  • P(3 hits) = p₁ × p₂ × p₃

Scoring System

  • 1 hit → 25 points
  • 2 hits → 50 points
  • 3 hits → 100 points
The scoring is nonlinear — getting all 3 correct is disproportionately valuable.

2. Strategy by Goal

🟢 Maximize Hot Streaks (Consistency)

  • Pick players from different teams/games
  • Maximize independence
  • Use highest probability players

Why: Maximizes probability of at least one goal each day.

Result: Best for 7-day streak rewards, lower leaderboard upside.

🔵 Maximize Expected Points (Best Overall EV)

  • Use high-probability players
  • Include mild correlation (same team/line)

Why: Correlation increases chances of 2 and 3 hits.

Result: Strong balance of consistency and upside.

🔴 Maximize Leaderboard Wins (High Variance)

  • Stack 2–3 players from same team
  • Target high-scoring games

Why: You need spike days (100 points) to win.

Result: High risk, high reward strategy.

3. Strategy Comparison

Strategy Correlation Floor Ceiling Best For
Independent Picks Low High Low Hot streaks
Hybrid (Balanced) Medium Medium Medium Best overall EV
Stacked Picks High Low High Leaderboard wins

4. Optimal Hybrid Strategy

Best overall approach:
Pick 2 correlated players + 1 independent player
  • 2 players from same team (line or power play)
  • 1 player from a different game

Benefits:

  • Maintains streak probability
  • Improves 2-hit and 3-hit outcomes
  • Balances risk and reward

5. Practical Daily Algorithm

  1. Convert odds → probabilities (de-vig)
  2. Rank players by probability
  3. Identify high-scoring teams
  4. Select:
    • Pick 1: Highest probability player
    • Pick 2: Correlated teammate
    • Pick 3: Best independent player

6. Advanced Edge

  • Use team implied goal totals
  • Target weak defenses and goalies
  • Favor high-tempo games
  • Slightly pivot from obvious picks for leaderboard edge

7. Final Recommendation

Best Overall Strategy:
2 correlated players + 1 independent player
  • Want streak rewards → fully independent
  • Want max total points → hybrid strategy
  • Want to win leaderboard → aggressive stacking
