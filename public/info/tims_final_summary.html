Tims Hockey Challenge Strategy Analysis

Key Strategies

  • Independent: Players from different games (best for streaks)
  • Hybrid: 2 same team + 1 independent (best EV)
  • Stack: 3 same team (best for leaderboard)
  • Opposing: Players from both teams (generally worst except 1-game streak case)

Baseline (Top Picks Example: 40% / 30% / 20%)

  • P(≥1 hit): ~72%
  • P(3 hits): ~3%
  • Expected Value: ~39.5 points

Strategy Comparison

Strategy EV P(≥1) P(3) Best Use
Independent Medium Highest Low Streaks
Hybrid Best Medium Medium-High Balanced / EV
Stack High Low Highest Leaderboard
Opposing Low Medium Lowest Only 1-game streak case

Correlation Threshold Formula

Use this to decide if lower probabilities are worth it:

(p1'/p1) × (p2'/p2) × (p3'/p3) ≥ 1 / C

  • Hybrid: C ≈ 1.3 → threshold ≈ 0.77
  • Stack: C ≈ 1.8 → threshold ≈ 0.56
  • Opposing: C ≈ 0.85 → avoid

Special Cases

2 Games (Streak)

  • Pick 1 from each game
  • 3rd pick from same team as strongest player

1 Game (Streak)

  • Pick from both teams
  • Prefer 2 from stronger team + 1 from opponent

Final Takeaways

  • Correlation drives EV and leaderboard success
  • Independence maximizes streak probability
  • Opposing picks reduce multi-hit outcomes
  • Pick 3 is the most important lever
